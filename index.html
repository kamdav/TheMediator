"Getting to Yes" … efficiently professionally kindly

In family mediation, Susan ...

  • Recognises that you are the experts about your own family so you make the decisions
  • Nurtures dignity in conflict resolution, conflict does not have to be ugly
  • Calms conflict so stress is reduced for you, your former partner and indirectly for your children and family
  • Places the interests of your children as a priority; works to assist you as parents prepare your children for the changes to come and meet their needs
  • Guides the adversarial nature of separation and divorce into a genuine opportunity for growth
  • Provides facilitation, information, support, skills and a process to assist you and your former partner to reach mutually agreed solutions to some of the most important and often the most challenging situations in your life, whether about your children, finances or property

You … are in control of the pace and the content of mediation; you decide the issues to be discussed. In litigation the courts decide your future; going to court is expensive; it’s out of your control; it’s emotionally draining and it impacts adversely everyone in the family
